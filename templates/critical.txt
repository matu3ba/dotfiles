critical is usual refered to as
* safety-critical
* security-critical

critical system
* highly reliable
* retain reliability during evolvement without incurring prohibitive costs
- fail-safe (become safe, when not operating)
- fail-operational (still working, when control system fails)
- fail-soft (still working with degraded functionality)
- fail-secure (maintain max security when not operating)
- fail-passive (continue to operate when failure)
- fault-tolerant (redundancy for keeping operation/avoid failure when faults are induced)

2 strictly specified types
1. safety critical (failure/malfunction leading to death/injury, loss/damage of equipment/property, environment harm)
* ISO 26262
* more information on https://public-docs.ferrocene.dev/main/index.html
* https://public-docs.ferrocene.dev/main/qualification/evaluation-plan/index.html
  - automotive [ISO 26262:2018] (ASIL D/TCL 3),
  - industrial [IEC 61508:2010] (class T3)
  - medical software [IEC 62304:2006 + AMD 1:2015]
  - automotive [ISO 26262:2018] (ASIL B)
  - industrial [IEC 61508:2010] (SIL 2)

2. security critical (unfortunately no widely accepted short definition)
* suggestion: Preventing or reducing the probability systems from threats that can lead to
unauthorized information access, unlawful use, disclosure, disruption,
deletion, corruption, modification, inspection, recording, devaluation, theft,
or damage to hardware, software (computer+network), communication channels, or
data, as well as to the disruption or misdirection of the services they
provide.
* CIA triad (confidentiality, integrity, availability)
* O-ISM3 as maturity model
* creative commons

2 loosely unspecified types
1. mission critical
* essential to survival of business/organization
2. business critical
* not necessary during incidents [situation that might be, or could lead to, a disruption, loss, emergency or crisis]
